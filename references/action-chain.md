# Action Chain

Use this reference for fights, impacts, escapes, falls, monster attacks, weapon actions, superpowers, and any motion where body mechanics or physical causality matters.

## Definition

A fight is not a martial arts display; it is designed conflict behavior.

Every conflict action must be built on an action chain:

`pre-contact state -> preparation -> force generation -> force path -> contact point -> contact impact -> attacker feedback -> defender/victim feedback -> environment interaction -> residue/tail state`

The backend must know the complete chain. The final shot may show only selected nodes.

## Three Layers

1. **Action-chain base**: body state, support point, center of gravity, force source, trajectory, contact, feedback, environment.
2. **Camera expression**: composition, shot size, camera, lens, motion, obstruction, edit, readability, information release.
3. **Style/memory**: director temperament, violence treatment, pressure, delayed payoff, memory point.

Style can only stand on the first two layers.

## Action Chain Nodes

### 1. Pre-Contact State

Define:

- character position and frame position
- depth layer
- distance and facing
- stance, support point, center of gravity
- injury, weapon state, environment obstacle, usable space

### 2. Preparation

The body preloads for force or defense:

- shoulder sinks
- knees bend
- hip compresses
- elbow retracts
- waist twists
- foot grips
- fingers tighten
- weapon draws back
- breath holds
- gaze locks

Untrained fighting also has preparation: neck shrinking, shoulders lifting messily, body lunging, fists too high, unstable footwork.

### 3. Force Generation

Realistic force sources:

- foot pushes ground
- knee/hip extension
- waist/hip rotation
- shoulder/elbow transfer
- body weight drops
- inertia forward
- wall pushback
- grabbing/pulling traction
- weapon inertia

Supernatural force sources still need rules:

- pressure jet
- mechanical amplification
- electromagnetic pull
- spiritual compression
- extended blade aura
- stored array energy
- abnormal gravity
- psychic interference

Supernatural means the force source changes, not that physics disappears.

### 4. Force Path

Define:

- start point
- path
- endpoint
- speed change
- direction
- obstruction
- residual momentum direction

Paths include body, weapon, and energy. A fist, knee, shoulder, blade, shield, mechanical arm, electric arc, gas pressure, or shock wave all need a path.

### 5. Contact Point

The contact point is the blast center.

Specify:

- who contacts whom
- which body part or object contacts
- where on the target
- contact angle
- whether blocked
- whether sliding, stuck, pressing, bouncing, cutting, piercing, or penetrating
- contact type: strike, cut, stab, friction, compression, grab, drag, parry, collision, trip, pin

Bad: “A punch hits the enemy.”

Better: “The second and third knuckle area of the right fist lands under the opponent's left cheekbone; sweat and lateral movement make the fist slip slightly, compressing skin and throwing a short burst of sweat.”

### 6. Contact Impact

Visible feedback must match force level.

Realistic impacts:

- skin compression
- muscle tremor
- fabric fold
- sweat spray
- shoulder knocked aside
- foot displacement
- breath interruption
- eye defocus

Weapon impacts:

- sparks
- chips
- blade rebound
- grip vibration
- metal deformation
- handle crack
- cloth strip motion

Supernatural impacts:

- electric arc jumping along contact surface
- ground-hugging air wave
- light leaking from cracks
- delayed rock splitting
- monster skin ripple

Do not make an ordinary punch create disaster-level shockwaves.

### 7. Attacker Feedback

The attacker pays a price:

- wrist recoil
- shoulder pulled back
- center of gravity drops forward
- foot slips
- breath breaks
- weapon shocks hand
- muscle cramps
- old wound pulls
- ability backlash
- device overload

Without attacker feedback, the character feels weightless.

### 8. Victim/Defender Feedback

Layer feedback:

- Local: head turns, shoulder collapses, abdomen folds, forearm pushed back, fingers loosen, knee bends.
- Whole-body: retreat, slip, kneel, hit wall, roll, pinned, body dragged off line.
- Physiological: cough, blood choke, eyes lose focus, breath interruption, reflex curl, brief stiffness.
- Defense: block structure breaks, footwork misplaces, guard arm pushed back, weapon drops, body pulled.

Untrained defense should show failure: flailing guard, covering head, eyes closed, back turned, clutching clothes, being dragged by inertia.

### 9. Environment Interaction

Environment interaction runs through the whole chain:

- Before: foot steps in water, clothes catch wire, back touches wall, palm finds broken brick.
- During load: foot slips, wall provides pushback, puddle ripples under pressure.
- During force: shoe scrapes mud, blade drags on wall, airflow rolls dust, stone kicks away.
- On contact: panel vibrates, water bursts, pipe shakes, light flickers, barrel rings.
- During feedback: body hits barrel, knee scrapes ground, weapon drops into water, brick crushes.

Environment participation levels:

0. Background only
1. Light and mood
2. Obstruction
3. Affects action stability
4. Participates in attack/defense
5. Determines outcome

### 10. Residue / Tail State

Actions leave traces:

- duration: 0.2s, 0.5s, 1s, cross-shot
- type: ripple, dust, fragments, blood drops, sparks, steam, electric arc, cloth swing, weapon vibration
- direction: spreading on ground, rolling into depth, falling from above, sliding along wall
- function: prove what happened, connect next shot, delay understanding, add weight

## Fight Type Parameters

- Professional martial arts: stable support, economical movement, clear offense/defense, strong center control.
- Untrained street fight: rough movement, overforce, unstable weight, grabbing, errors, inefficient action.
- Weapon fight: distance, edge line, weapon inertia, parry point, disarm risk.
- Supernatural fight: source, trigger, path, price, environment feedback.
- Monster fight: scale difference, pressure path, evasion route, ground feedback, destruction.
- Escape fight: goal is not victory, but pressure release or escape window.
- Suppression fight: one side advances; the other loses position and options.
- Multi-person chaos: keep one main action chain; others become background interference.

## Display Modes

Not every action chain needs full display.

- Full chain: key blow, instructional action, first ability reveal, major reversal.
- Half chain: medium action, normal exchange, clear node in continuous fight.
- Node display: contact and feedback only; useful for fast cuts.
- Result display: residue only; useful for speed, mystery, overwhelming power.
- Reaction display: victim, defender, bystander, environment reaction.
- Sound display: action partly off-screen, completed by sound and environmental feedback.

## Body Mechanics

### Support Points

No support, no believable force. Support can be both feet, one foot, palm, knee, shoulder/back on wall, weapon on ground, enemy body, temporary prop, or lost support.

Rules:

- unstable support makes actions shorter, less efficient, and easier to lose control
- severe injury reduces complete force actions

### Center of Gravity

- High: display, threat, preparing attack.
- Medium: normal fighting.
- Low: defense, escape, ground movement.
- Biased: injured, pushed, slipping.
- Out of control: hit, falling, rolling.
- Secondary correction: trying to recover after impact.

### Attack Lines

Common lines:

- horizontal sweep
- diagonal upward strike
- diagonal downward chop
- straight center thrust
- low leg sweep
- close elbow
- sinking shoulder ram
- backhand pull-cut
- short point stab
- over-shoulder smash

Each line needs start, endpoint, acting part, obstruction point, and residual momentum.

## Camera Parameters for Action

Frame position must clarify:

- where the main person is in the frame
- depth layer
- relative position to others and environment
- distance, facing, obstruction
- key prop position

Shot size:

- Wide: spatial relation and distance.
- Medium: body action and offense/defense.
- Close: contact point, expression, physiology.
- Insert/detail: eye, hand, weapon, wound, valve, arc, fragment.

Camera:

- side/front-side to see attack trajectory
- low angle for pressure
- ground follow or rear follow for escape
- obstruction gap or reaction shot for unseen threat

Camera involvement and readability trade off. High involvement lowers action readability. Do not demand extreme chaos and perfect clarity simultaneously.

## VFX and Sound

Effects need a source:

- metal sparks from friction
- electric arc from damaged interface
- steam from valve or broken pipe
- water from puddle impact
- dust from ground/stone contact
- blood from wound
- device overload from stress

Sound must sync with action chain:

- main sound: most important action sound
- secondary sound: body or environment support
- tail sound: residual sound that carries across time or cut

Examples:

- main: fist landing on forearm
- secondary: shoe spreading water film
- tail: barrel ringing twice after impact

## Conflict Checks

- Injured, exhausted, or unsupported characters cannot perform repeated clean advanced actions.
- Untrained fighting should not become clean martial arts routine.
- Escape fights should not become heroic front-facing duels.
- High chaos handheld cannot make every action perfectly readable.
- Extreme close-ups cannot show full spatial relations.
- Ordinary hits cannot cause disaster feedback.
- Effects must have source, direction, contact, and residue.
- Environment cannot reset between shots.
- One shot should carry one main action chain or one main information anchor.
